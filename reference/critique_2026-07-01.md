# Critique: ROADMAP.md, BUILD_PLAN.md, and the codebase (2026-07-01)

**What this is:** a point-in-time critique of the two planning docs and the codebase,
produced by the build agent from a full read of ROADMAP.md/BUILD_PLAN.md/MEMORY.md plus
four delegated code/test/engine/docs surveys. Branch at time of writing:
`deterministic-expansion-strategy`, with the map-walker full-corpus work uncommitted.
Grill-me targets at the bottom are the recommended stress-test sessions.

**TL;DR:** Engineering discipline is genuinely strong — clean layering, fail-loud
culture that's tested rather than aspirational, a small well-fenced engine divergence,
an exemplary PBS test layer. The weaknesses are almost all *drift*: the planning docs
no longer describe what's being built, the declared "spine" work (deterministic
transpiler + fork index) has zero code behind it two weeks after being declared top
priority, and the newest/largest surfaces (phase5, map viewer, assembly paths) are the
least tested. The single biggest unexamined risk is the ROM budget: **86.59% of 32MB
with no scripts, no NPCs, no audio, and 21 maps already cut.**

---

## ROADMAP.md

### Strengths

- The two-agent distinction (build agent vs. conversion agent) is the doc's best idea —
  a real confusion class, killed with a comparison table before it can bite.
- Phase 0's "define playable before writing converters" discipline was actually
  followed, and the success criteria are concrete (leave town, one trainer battle,
  Nuclear type effectiveness, save persists, 30 crash-free minutes).
- It's honestly a *living* document: the 2026-06-18 pivot is recorded in place with a
  "Historical note (superseded)" block instead of being silently rewritten, so the
  reasoning trail survives.
- The Known Pitfalls section is earned, not generic — `healparty`/`HealPlayerParty`,
  encoding traps, and the legal boundary are all lessons from this project's own
  history.

### Weaknesses

- **The Phase 4 section is internally contradictory.** "Why this needs an LLM, not a
  script" (arguing rule-based output would be "unreadable garbage") sits directly above
  the pivot text saying the LLM is retired and a rule-based transpiler is the spine.
  §4.2 still calls the backend abstraction "the single most important architectural
  decision in this phase," §4.5 and "Bulk-run mechanics" describe the retired bulk run
  in present tense, and the Phase 4 exit criteria are entirely bulk-run-shaped
  ("~10% sample of bulk output manually reviewed"). A newcomer reading top-to-bottom
  gets two incompatible plans.
- **Phase 5's task list predates its own approach section.** The approach says
  "quantize real art" (decided 2026-06-18), but tasks 5.1/5.4 still describe the manual
  tile-mapping table and hand-wiring connections in Porymap — both superseded by the
  quantizer and computed warp pairing.
- **The vertical-slice model was bolted on, not integrated.** The doc says slices
  "supersede strict phase ordering," but Phases 5–8 and every exit-criteria checklist
  still assume horizontal order. In practice "what to build next" now lives in
  BUILD_PLAN/MEMORY, which quietly falsifies the CLAUDE.md rule that "the roadmap wins
  for *what*."
- Deferred exit criteria aren't marked in place — Phase 2's #3/#4 were deferred to
  Phase 7 (per MEMORY), but the checklist still shows them as pending Phase 2 items.
- Duration estimates ("Phase 6: 1–2 weeks") are fiction under the slice model and no
  actuals are tracked against them.

## BUILD_PLAN.md

### Strengths

- Consolidating 19 scattered root planning docs into one "current how" doc was real
  hygiene, and the ROADMAP/BUILD_PLAN authority split (what vs. how) is a good contract
  on paper.
- The pivot rationale is *measured, not vibes*: the ~36% byte-identical pre-filter
  figure, the compile-clean-but-wrong error taxonomy, and the observation that all
  slice playability blockers were deterministic-pipeline gaps.
- The per-slice loop is concrete and has a falsifiable guardrail: "slice 2 must need
  data/config changes, not code changes."
- §4's fork-index guard design is precise in both directions and honest that the
  reverse gate is curation, not automation. §3.3 "Honest limits" is the kind of candor
  most plans lack.

### Weaknesses

- **Plan-reality inversion — the big one.** §3 (transpiler) and §4 (fork index) are
  build steps 1 and 2, and MEMORY's "Next tasks" list them as #1 and #2. Verified
  directly: **no `transpile_page`, no capability-index module exists anywhere in
  `src/` or `scripts/`.** Meanwhile the actual last ~2 weeks of work — the map walker
  debug ROM, `phase5.convert_all`, per-map tileset packing, the full-corpus 177-map
  build, the map viewer — appears *nowhere* in BUILD_PLAN. The walker's plan lives in
  `reference/map_walker_plan.md`, whose status line still reads "Proposed (awaiting
  build greenlight)" despite two committed phases. The "single current-how doc" no
  longer describes the current how; the de facto plan is MEMORY.md's Current Phase
  section, which has ballooned into dense paragraph-blobs that even now lag reality
  (top section describes the 8-map batch; the latest session summary records full
  corpus).
- §6's slice status is stale (warps "needs re-assemble to confirm," layout "OPEN —
  by-design v1") — the image pipeline has since shipped and been validated. §8's
  tracked obligation says the fork divergences are "uncommitted" — they've been
  committed since `be00b774`, and the walker added six more touch-points.
- **No ROM-budget accounting anywhere**, despite it being the binding constraint at
  corpus scale (16 maps over the tile budget, stock-map stubbing required just to link,
  86.59% full with no content). The plan treats the budget as a per-tileset guard, not
  a system-level ledger.
- The walker-before-transpiler sequencing may well be *correct* (validate
  art/warps/map-identity at corpus scale before investing in events) — but it's an
  undocumented reversal of the plan's own ordering. Nobody decided it on paper.

## Codebase

### Strengths

- **Layering is genuinely clean.** Exactly two cross-package imports among the four
  packages, both one-directional and sensible. Zero typing-import violations, zero
  `os.path`, `encoding="utf-8"` everywhere (AST-verified); `src/` is ruff-clean under
  the project's own config while `scripts/` (60 findings) shows the quality bar is a
  maintained distinction, not accident.
- **Fail-loud is a tested behavior, not a slogan** — `pytest.raises` with message
  matches across `map_set`, PBS converters, and the map-constants collision path; the
  emit budget guard raises before writing artifacts. The Moki Town test failures exist
  *because* the system failed loud, which is the design working.
- **The PBS layer is exemplary**: every module has round-trip + golden fixture +
  Uranium-quirk edge tests, exactly as CLAUDE.md §4.6 demands.
- **The engine divergence is small and disciplined**: ~150 fenced lines across 10
  touch-points plus one wholly new 761-line file (`uranium_map_walker.c`) with only 3
  exported symbols, zero TODO/HACK debt, and unusually good "why here, not there"
  comments (the `DoMapLoadLoop` callback trap is documented at the site).
- The suite is fast (591 tests, ~12s) and hermetic where it matters — no test spawns
  `claude` or hits the network.
- Non-obvious decisions are documented at the point of complexity (the `quantize.py`
  bin-packing-not-k-means docstring cites the actual "paths turned green" bug that
  motivated it).

### Weaknesses

- **Two independently maintained fork-assembly paths.** `scripts/assemble_pathfinder.py`
  and `tileset_converter/phase5.py` duplicate the `map_groups.gen.json` writer
  (assemble_pathfinder.py:311-326 vs phase5.py:271-278), the `uranium_includes.inc`
  writer (assemble_pathfinder.py:382-413 vs phase5.py:296-307, same banner text), and
  layout/header glue, with neither importing the other. Any overlay-format change must
  be made twice and can drift silently between the slice path and the corpus path.
- **The single-source-of-truth story has regressed.** `map_set.py`'s docstring claims
  to have replaced five hardcoded copies of `[49, 48, 32]`; at least six scripts still
  hardcode it (`run_slice.py:66`, `pathfinder_warp_trace.py:19`,
  `pathfinder_tile_census.py:25`, `triage_slice.py:12`, `slice_tile_census.py:10`,
  `render_slice_tiles.py:19`), and `stage_slice_scripts.py` imports `SLICE_MAP_IDS` on
  line 43 then defines an independent `ALLOWED_MAPS = {49, 48, 32}` on line 46.
- **Silent-swallow spots contradict the fail-loud rule at exactly the wrong layer.**
  `deterministic.py:813-839` bare-`except`s reference-data loads (a typo'd path makes
  classifiers silently stop firing, no log); `_naming.load_fork_constants` (lines
  64-74) returns an empty set on a bad fork path, silently disabling constant
  validation across *every* PBS converter; `map_constants.load_vanilla_map_consts`
  (lines 314-322) ignores an explicit `fork_path=None` and reads the env anyway — the
  direct cause of two current test failures. Also `trainers.py:301` falls back to a raw
  int on an unknown gender enum.
- **Test-suite integrity is quietly environmental.** The real local result is **3
  failures, not the "2 known" recorded in MEMORY** — the third,
  `test_real_slice_map032_prunes_clean` (test_assembly.py:367), has a different root
  cause: staged `output/` artifacts two weeks out of sync with each other
  (`Map032.pory` mtime Jun 17 vs `MokiTown/map.json` mtime Jul 1). CI runs with no env
  configured and exercises a materially weaker suite (513 passed / 76 skipped when the
  env vars don't resolve) and would have caught neither failure class. One
  poryscript-compile test (`test_deterministic.py:221` `test_wait_se_output_compiles`)
  is missing the `skipif` guard its 8 siblings have and hard-fails without the binary.
  Normalizing "known failures" is the exact slope CLAUDE.md §8 warns about.
- **Coverage gaps sit precisely on the newest, biggest surfaces**:
  `phase5.convert_all`/`_assemble_fork` (the production corpus driver) untested beyond
  `_stub_stock_layout_bins`; `pipeline.py`'s Click commands never invoked by any test;
  `map_viewer_common.py` — at 1,634 lines the largest Python file in the repo, shared
  by three scripts, importing the underscore-private `_render_column` across the
  script→src boundary (map_viewer_common.py:23) — has zero tests;
  `assemble_pathfinder.py` zero; `connections.py` zero (11 skipped stubs).
- **~2,000 LOC of LLM-era machinery** (orchestrator.py 1,000 + backends/ 819 + lane.py
  + run_report.py) is co-located with the deterministic spine in `conversion_agent/`
  with nothing marking spine vs. legacy tail tool for a new reader — and it's sized for
  an unattended 199-map sweep that the project's own docs say no longer happens.
- **Documentation drift is systemic, not incidental**: `engine/RPG2GBA_VENDOR.md`
  omits the entire map-walker divergence (the largest addition to the vendored tree);
  `reference/conversion_walkthrough.md` presents the retired Opus loop as *the* Phase 4
  process; `reference/map_inspector_spec.md`'s "supersedes compare_collision.py" claim
  is contradicted by the live import graph (map_viewer_common.py:41 imports it);
  `assembly_smoke.py`/`moveroute_coverage.py`/`assemble_pathfinder.py` docstrings cite
  planning docs at root paths that moved to `reference/archive/`;
  `reference/map_walker_plan.md` still says "Proposed."
- **Operational hygiene wobbles**: a large multi-layer uncommitted working set
  (engine C + scripts + src + reference, 21 modified + 6 untracked files) against a
  slice-loop rule that says "commit one logical unit"; the checked-in engine default
  `URANIUM_MAP_WALKER TRUE` (include/config/uranium_walker.h) means a naive `make`
  produces the debug walker, not the game; a known 1-line IWRAM fix
  (`sMenuListItems` → `EWRAM_DATA`, frees ~28KB) recorded in memory but not applied;
  empty untracked `scratchpad.txt` at root.
- Complexity hotspots concentrate in the graphics path: `emit_tileset` (emit.py:245,
  186 lines, budget guard buried mid-function), `build_slice_tilesets`
  (build_slice_tilesets.py:172, 176 lines, depth-4 nesting), `classify_trainer_battle`
  (deterministic.py:620, regex-parsing embedded Ruby), and the O(k²) agglomerative
  quantizer (quantize.py:161) that's also the measured ~22s viewer bottleneck.

## Grill-me targets, ranked

Each phrased as the question the session should answer.

> **Re-ranked 2026-07-01** after the user clarified the plan: most vanilla
> Emerald/expansion content gets pulled and replaced with Uranium content. That
> demotes raw ROM size (subtraction plausibly frees 8–12MB — nine gens of species
> data/sprites/cries plus Hoenn maps/scripts/music — vs ~4–6MB of Uranium content
> still to add; the stock-layout stubbing already proved the approach). The budget
> question survives as the *subtraction plan* target at #3.

1. **The missing spine.** "The transpiler and fork index have been 'next tasks #1 and
   #2' since June 18 and zero code exists. Is walker-first a deliberate resequencing or
   drift? What triggers transpiler work, and is the frozen-Opus differential oracle
   still trustworthy after everything that's changed?" **(RESOLVED — grill session
   held; decision record: `reference/grill_spine_2026-07-02.md`.)**
2. **Walker-to-product transfer.** "The walker validates art and warps with collision
   disabled, NPCs suppressed, and scripts skipped. Which defect classes can it not see,
   and what does Checkpoint 2 actually certify about the real game ROM?" Also: the §9
   boot gates keep queueing (the 2026-06-21 slice boot, Checkpoint 1, Checkpoint 2) —
   has any been formally walked and signed off? **(RESOLVED — Checkpoint 2 formally
   walked + signed off 2026-07-02 (8-map batch; full corpus sampled only, follow-up
   open). Certification statement + not-certified ledger + per-class dispositions:
   `reference/walker_checkpoint2_findings.md`.)**
3. **The Emerald-subtraction plan (was: ROM budget).** "What exactly gets pulled, by
   what mechanism, and what does the ledger say?" Species via the expansion's supported
   `P_FAMILY_*`/gen toggles is cheap; Hoenn maps/scripts have engine coupling (`MAP_*`
   refs in C specials, link-time script labels, PC/Frontier wiring) where the
   repoint-don't-delete pattern (cf. `_stub_stock_layout_bins`) is the proven move.
   Decide config-driven exclusion vs surgical deletion (the latter kills the
   pristine-vendor + sentinel-fence contract in `RPG2GBA_VENDOR.md`). Note this plan
   *is* the deferred Phase 2 exit criteria (roster replacement resolves the vanilla
   constant collisions, decision 2026-05-25). Get a real ledger from the linker `.map`
   — and watch the one big Uranium-side line item: per-map tileset packing gave up
   cross-map dedup (likely 6–10MB across 177 maps).
4. **Definition of done.** "Is the target still the Phase 0 thirty-minute MVP, or a
   full 199-map playable game? What's the acceptance story for the 21 excluded maps and
   for audio ('Phase 8 nice-to-have' currently means a silent game)?" The slice model
   widens the frontier indefinitely without ever forcing this decision.
5. **The flag budget and save format (ROADMAP §7.4).** "~860 self-switch flags plus
   temp switches exceed vanilla's free flag space, the default bases 'assemble but are
   not runtime-correct,' and this is deferred to Phase 7 — while save persistence is a
   Phase 0 success criterion. Is deferral actually safe?"
6. **CI and known-failure discipline.** "CI runs a weaker suite than the desk does, the
   '2 known failures' are actually 3 with two distinct root causes, and env-gated tests
   silently skip. What invariant is CI supposed to protect?"
7. **Retirement plan for the dual assembly path.** "When does `assemble_pathfinder.py`
   die? Full-fidelity assembly (events + NPCs + scripts) currently lives only in the
   slice script while `phase5` is warps-only — which one grows into the real Phase 7
   assembler?"
8. **The LLM tail's real size.** "When the transpiler exists, how big is the
   irreducible queue actually expected to be (the move-route HARD tail alone is 35% of
   1,191 events), and who does that work at what pace?"
